# TOP-15 METROS for Elite Service Hub Expansion

**Analysis Date:** February 16, 2026  
**Objective:** Identify top 15 US metros for contractor lead generation based on data-driven scoring

---

## Methodology

Each metro area was scored based on four weighted factors:

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Median Household Income** | 40% | Higher income = more spending power for home improvements |
| **Construction Spending/Permits** | 30% | More construction = more lead opportunities |
| **Population Growth Rate** | 20% | Faster growth = expanding market |
| **Competition Density** | 10% | Less competition = easier market penetration |

**Data Sources:**
- U.S. Census Bureau (2024 population estimates, building permits)
- American Community Survey (median household income)
- Associated General Contractors of America (construction employment)
- Construction industry reports (spending data)

## Key Takeaways

### Regional Patterns
- **Texas dominates:** 3 Texas metros in top 5, driven by massive new construction and business-friendly environment
- **California premium markets:** Bay Area metros score high on income and low competition despite population declines
- **Sun Belt growth:** Florida, Arizona, and North Carolina metros show strong population growth
- **Tech hubs:** Seattle, Austin, and Raleigh benefit from tech sector wealth

### Market Segmentation

| Market Type | Best Metros | Contractor Focus |
|-------------|--------------|------------------|
| **New Construction** | Dallas, Houston, Phoenix | Production builders, HVAC, roofing, electrical |
| **Premium Renovation** | San Jose, San Francisco, Seattle | High-end finishes, smart home, energy efficiency |
| **Steady Growth** | DC, Denver, Atlanta | Both residential and commercial projects |
| **Fast-Growing** | Austin, Orlando, Raleigh | New construction + first-time buyer projects |

### Strategic Recommendations

1. **Priority Entry:** Start with Dallas-Fort Worth (#1) for scale and Houston (#5) for volume
2. **Premium Market:** Add San Jose (#3) or San Francisco (#2) for high-value leads
3. **Growth Play:** Austin (#7) and Raleigh (#9) offer growth + manageable competition
4. **Weather Specialization:** Phoenix (#10) for HVAC, Tampa (#15) for hurricane prep

## Data Notes

- **Population:** 2024 Census Bureau estimates
- **Median Income:** 2024 American Community Survey data
- **Construction Spending:** 2024 total construction put-in-place, includes residential, commercial, and public
- **Building Permits:** 2024 Census Building Permits Survey, includes all new residential units
- **Population Growth:** 2023-2024 annual growth rate
- **Competition Density:** Estimated based on construction employment per capita, market saturation, and licensing data

*Analysis compiled February 16, 2026. Sources cited above. All figures rounded for readability.*
