## 2. STEP-BY-STEP FORMULATION

### Mixing Ratios (By Weight)

#### Base Epoxy Mix (Marble Base)
```
Part A (Epoxy Resin): 100 parts (40 lbs for 5 gallons)
Part B (Hardener): 35-40 parts (14-16 lbs for EPN system)
Ceramic Microspheres: 0.5-1.0 parts (0.2-0.4 lbs)
Air Release Agent: 0.5 parts (0.2 lbs)
```

#### For Marble Effect Veining
```
White Base: 80% of total mix
Colored Veins: 20% of total mix (distributed)
Pigment Loading: 2-5% by weight for veins
```

### Order of Mixing

1. **Preparation (15 mins)**
   - Wear nitrile gloves, safety glasses, and respirator (NIOSH P100)
   - Ensure workspace is 75-85°F and low humidity (<60%)
   - Clean surface with denatured alcohol

2. **Base Mix (10 mins)**
   - Weigh Part A resin into clean mixing bucket
   - Add ceramic microspheres slowly while stirring
   - Add air release agent and mix thoroughly
   - Scrape sides and bottom to ensure uniform dispersion

3. **Pigment Preparation (5 mins)**
   - **White Base**: Add 1-2% titanium white to main mix
   - **Vein Colors**: Prepare separate small batches:
     - Grey veins: 1% black + 0.5% white pigment
     - Gold veins: 2% gold mica + 0.5% brown
     - Pearl veins: 2% pearl mica + 0.5% white

4. **Hardener Addition (5 mins)**
   - Add Part B hardener to main mix
   - Mix for exactly 2-3 minutes (don't overmix)
   - **Pot Life**: 20-30 minutes at 75°F

5. **Marble Effect Creation (15 mins)**
   - Pour 80% white base onto surface
   - Randomly drizzle colored vein mixtures
   - Use wooden stick or paddle to create veining
   - Drag and swirl for marble pattern
   - **Work Time**: 15-20 minutes before gel

### Curing Schedule
- **Initial Cure**: 6-8 hours (tack-free)
- **Light Use**: 24 hours
- **Full Cure**: 72 hours
- **Polyaspartic Topcoat**: Apply after 24 hours

### Safety Equipment Required
- Nitrile gloves (chemical resistant)
- Safety glasses with side shields
- NIOSH P100 respirator or equivalent
- Long-sleeved chemical-resistant clothing
- Adequate ventilation (20+ CFM exhaust)

## 3. COLOR MIXING GUIDE

### Pigment Types and Effects

#### Mica Powders (for Metallic/Pearl Effects)
- **3M™ Perfect Pearls**: Excellent UV stability
- **Coastal Business Supplies**: Wide color range
- **Loading**: 2-5% by weight for opaque, 0.5-1% for subtle

#### Liquid Pigments (for Base Colors)
- **TiO2 (Titanium Dioxide)**: White base
- **Carbon Black**: Grey/black veins
- **Iron Oxides**: Earth tones for brown/gold veins

### Veining Technique

#### Preparation
1. **Clean Mix Buckets**: Any residue affects color
2. **Pre-mix Colors**: Have all vein colors ready before pouring
3. **Temperature Control**: Keep all materials at same temperature

#### Application Steps
1. **Base Pour (60% of volume)**
   - Pour white base first
   - Spread to 1/8" thickness

2. **Primary Veins (20% of volume)**
   - Use 2-3 different grey shades
   - Pour in flowing lines across surface
   - Use figure-8 motion for natural look

3. **Accent Veins (10% of volume)**
   - Add gold/pearl accents sparingly
   - Focus on high points and edges

4. **Blending (10% of volume)**
   - Small amount of clear resin
   - Lightly drag through veins to blend

### Mixing Ratios for Marble Effects

#### Carrara White Marble
```
Base White: 100% + 2% TiO2
Grey Veins: Base + 1% Carbon Black + 0.5% TiO2
Highlight Veins: Base + 0.3% Silver Mica
```

#### Calacatta Gold Marble
```
Base White: 100% + 1.5% TiO2
Gold Veins: Base + 2% Gold Mica + 0.5% Yellow Oxide
Grey Veins: Base + 0.8% Carbon Black + 0.3% TiO2
```

#### Modern Grey Marble
```
Base Grey: 100% + 0.5% Carbon Black + 0.2% TiO2
White Veins: Base + 3% TiO2
Black Veins: Base + 2% Carbon Black
```

## 5. TROUBLESHOOTING & TIPS

### Common Issues and Solutions

#### Bubbles/Pinholes
- **Cause**: Incomplete mixing, high humidity
- **Solution**: Use air release agent, proper torch technique

#### Yellowing
- **Cause**: UV exposure, low-quality resins
- **Solution**: Use UV-stable resins, apply polyaspartic topcoat

#### Poor Flow
- **Cause**: Low temperature, high viscosity
- **Solution**: Warm materials to 75-85°F, use flow additives

#### Inconsistent Color
- **Cause**: Incomplete pigment dispersion
- **Solution**: Pre-mix pigments in small amount of resin, use mechanical mixer

### Treasure Coast Specific Considerations

#### Climate Adaptations
- **High Humidity**: Use dehumidifier, wait for <60% RH
- **High Temperature**: Work early morning or late afternoon
- **Salt Air**: Ensure thorough surface preparation, use marine-grade primers

#### Local Regulations
- **VOC Requirements**: Use low-VOC polyaspartics for commercial applications
- **Disposal**: Follow local hazardous waste guidelines
